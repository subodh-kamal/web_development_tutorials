- **1752:** Benjamin Franklin demonstrated that lightning is a form of electricity using his famous kite experiment.
- **1800:** Alessandro Volta invented the first electric battery (the voltaic pile), enabling continuous electric current.
- Thomas Edison and Nikola Tesla played major roles in the practical generation, transmission, and use of electricity (DC vs AC systems).
- **1837:** Telegraph (used to send Morse code)
    
    - The **telegraph** is a communication device used to send messages over long distances using **electrical signals**. It was developed by **Samuel Morse** in **1837**. Messages were sent in the form of **dots and dashes**, known as **Morse code**. The telegraph was the first system to allow **instant long-distance communication** and laid the foundation for modern communication technologies such as the telephone and the internet.

- **March 1876:** Invention of **Telephone** by Alexander Graham Bell. On March 7, 1876, Bell received the first patent for the telephone.

- Just three days later, he successfully transmitted speech to his assistant, Thomas Watson. **First successful call:**
“Mr. Watson, come here, I want to see you.” 

- In **1895**, Guglielmo Marconi successfully used **radio waves** to send **wireless signals** over long distances.
